# Jabin Chen - Interview Answer Patterns

## Tell me about yourself

I am a full-stack developer based in Auckland with a Master of Information Technology with First Class Honours from the University of Auckland. My experience spans React, TypeScript, .NET, Node.js, cloud deployment, and AI-enabled products. The two examples that usually represent me best are Medimate, where I led a 4-person team building a healthcare product across iOS and web, and Schedora, which I built independently from a real operational problem in property management. What I think differentiates me is that I enjoy building end-to-end products that are technically solid but also clearly useful in the real world.

## What are your strongest projects?

The strongest two are usually Schedora and Medimate. Schedora is important because I built it solo from a real workflow pain point, which shows product ownership and full-stack capability. Medimate is important because it shows leadership, cross-platform frontend delivery, AI integration, accessibility thinking, and cloud deployment in a team setting.

## Why are you a strong fit for a full-stack role?

I am comfortable owning both the frontend and backend sides of delivery. On the frontend, I have strong experience with React, TypeScript, and modern UI work, and on the backend I have built APIs and systems with ASP.NET Core and Node.js. I also understand deployment, Docker, AWS, and CI/CD, so I can take features much closer to production rather than stopping at the UI layer.

## What is your cloud experience?

I have practical deployment experience with AWS and Azure, especially through Dockerized application stacks. In Medimate, I worked with AWS deployment, Nginx, and GitHub Actions CI/CD. In personal work like Schedora and the Financial Podcast System, I also used Docker-based deployment patterns and cloud integrations such as AWS EC2 and Azure App Service.

## What is your AI experience?

My AI experience is applied rather than purely research-focused. In Medimate, I helped design and implement an AI image-search workflow that identified medication from packaging photos and improved pharmacist workflow efficiency by 40 percent in UAT. In MusicChat, I integrated OpenAI for AI-driven recommendations and conversation. I have also built AI-related personal systems around summarization, podcast generation, and retrieval-based assistants.

## Tell me about a time you led something

One of the clearest examples is Medimate. I led a team of four developers while we built a multi-platform pharmacy product across SwiftUI and React. My role included coordinating delivery, reviewing work, and owning major frontend architecture decisions. What I learned there is that good leadership is not only task assignment, but making sure technical decisions stay aligned with user value and team momentum.

## Tell me about a project with real business impact

Medimate is a strong example because the work was tied to pharmacy operations rather than just a demo application. The AI image-search flow we implemented helped identify medication from packaging photos, connected that into the product catalog, and ultimately improved pharmacist workflow efficiency by 40 percent in UAT. I like that example because it combines technical design with a measurable operational outcome.

## Tell me about something you built from scratch

Schedora is the best example. I built it after seeing how much time and friction existed in manual property inspection scheduling and communication. I designed the backend in ASP.NET Core, built the frontend in React, integrated Google Calendar and Sheets, and structured the platform around practical operational workflows. That project reflects how I like to work: start from a real pain point, then design the software around solving it cleanly.

## How do you approach unfamiliar problems?

I usually start by clarifying the real constraint first, whether that is user workflow, system performance, delivery speed, or maintainability. Then I break the problem into smaller parts I can validate quickly. That approach has helped me in both team settings like Medimate and solo product work like Schedora, where I had to move between architecture, implementation, and practical deployment decisions.
